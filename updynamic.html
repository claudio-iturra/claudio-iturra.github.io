Coastal upwelling occurs when wind-driven Ekman transport moves surface waters offshore, causing deep, cold, nutrient-rich water to rise to the surface.

📐 Fundamental Equations

Ekman Transport

ME = τ / (ρ × f)
Where: ME = Ekman transport, τ = wind stress, ρ = water density, f = Coriolis parameter

Wind Stress

τ = ρair × CD × U10²
Where: ρair = air density, CD = drag coefficient, U10 = wind speed at 10m

Upwelling Velocity

w = (1/ρ) × (∂τ/∂x) / f
Where: w = vertical velocity, ∂τ/∂x = alongshore wind stress gradient

Coriolis Parameter

f = 2Ω sin(φ)
Where: Ω = Earth's rotation rate (7.27 × 10⁻⁵ rad/s), φ = latitude

Plume Intensity Index

PII = (ΔT × A × w) / (tmix)
Where: ΔT = temperature difference, A = plume area, w = upwelling velocity, tmix = mixing time
