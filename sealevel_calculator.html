üî¨ Sea Level Rise: Key Parameters Overview

Sea level rise is driven by multiple interconnected atmospheric and oceanographic processes. This system evaluates the primary contributors based on current scientific research.

üå°Ô∏è Thermal Expansion

Ocean water expands as it warms, contributing ~40% of current sea level rise.

Reference: Church, J.A. et al. (2013). Sea Level Change. In: Climate Change 2013: The Physical Science Basis. IPCC AR5.

üßä Ice Sheet Mass Loss

Greenland and Antarctic ice sheet melting contributes ~35% of sea level rise.

Reference: Shepherd, A. et al. (2018). Mass balance of the Antarctic Ice Sheet from 1992 to 2017. Nature, 558, 219-222.

üèîÔ∏è Glacier Mass Loss

Mountain glaciers and ice caps contribute ~20% of current sea level rise.

Reference: Zemp, M. et al. (2019). Global glacier mass changes and their contributions to sea-level rise from 1961 to 2016. Nature, 568, 382-386.

üåä Ocean Dynamics

Changes in ocean circulation patterns affect regional sea level variations.

Reference: Stammer, D. et al. (2013). Causes for contemporary regional sea level changes. Annual Review of Marine Science, 5, 21-46.
